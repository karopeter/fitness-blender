Fresh and Easy Salmon Tacos with
Guacamole & Broccoli Slaw

T here are many ways to make soft tacos but this recipe is one of our favorites for a delicious protein and
veggie packed fresh taco.

Salmon and guacamole? What's not to like about that? Combined with some colorful and curnchy broccoli
slaw, this makes a fresh and easy lunch option to bring in to work or prepare for a picnic.

Here we have combine all kinds of good fats to keep your energy up throughout the day. Salmon and
avocado both help turn this into a filling meal - though we recommend eating this with a side of beans or fresh
fruit for some extra carbohydrate and fiber.

What's cool about this recipes is the broccoli slaw add-on. Sure, you can buy it ready-made but it is really easy
to make at home and will definitely save you some money. Here we present you with a painless way to prepare
it at home. You can make abig batch and store it in your fridge so that you have some fresh veggies
throughout the whole week. You need just a food processor! We like to use the grater blade but you can
definitely just chop it into small chunk with a regular knife.

These delicious tacos are an awesome picnic food that are quick and easy to prepare. It is also a wonderful
and mfilling breakfast if you have a long and strenuous day ahead. Just give it a try and your body will thank you
with all the energy it bursts with!

Need help planning some healthy meals? Check out the FB 4 Week Meal Plan - Eat Real Food & Feel Great,
written by certified dietitians.

Type: Lunch, Dinner
Prep time: 15 min
Cook time: 15 min
Serves: 2-4 portions

Ingredients
4 soft tortillas (ideally, look for ones with the most simple, least processed ingredients)
2 salmon fillets
1 broccoli stem
1 carrot
5 cherry tomatoes
1 clove garlic
2 springs fresh cilanto
juice of 1/2 lime
1 cup mixed leafy greens
1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
1 tbsp olive oil
salt, pepper

Recipe:
1. Place the salmon filets on a baking tray lined with parchment paper.
2. Season with salt and pepper and roast for 7-8 minutes at 160 degress centigrates
3. To make the broccoli slaw; rinse the vegetables and cut out the florets from broccoli.
4. In a food processor with a grater tool roughly chop the cabbage, carrot and broccoli stem.
5. Transfer the grated veggies in a bowl. Season with vinegar and olive oil and stir to combine.
6. To make the gucamole, cut the avocado into small pieces and put it in a bowl.
7. Slightly press with a fork to make a rough puree; don't mind if there are still some small chunks.
8. Add finely chopped cherry tomatoes, garlic clove and cilantro leaves.
9. Season with salt and pepper and squeeze the juice of 1/2 lime. Stir everything well to combine and season.
10. To assemble take a tortila and place 2 tablespoon of guacamole at the bottom.
10. Spread some of the broccoli slaw.
11. Add some chunks of the salmon and finish with some greens.
12. Roll into a tortila and enjoy.

Note: You can prep these in advance and store them for up to 3 days in an airtight container in the fridge

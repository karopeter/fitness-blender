HIIT and Kickboxing Cardio Plus Abs - Home
Cardio and Abs Workout

Ready to get your heart rate up, and your muscles sore? This HIIT cardio and cardio kickboxing routine with an
abs burnout round is a greate home workout to burn calories, impove endurance and coordination all without
having to set foot in a gym. No equioment means you can do this routine anywhere you have the space and an
internet connection. Warn and sunny outside? Do it at your local park. Rainy? Do it in your living room.
Travelling? Do it your hotel room.

We have combine two of out favorite types of carido exercises (that also challenge those muscles) and put it
with a quick abs routine to make a great sweat dripping routine. Because this workout videos does not use any
equipment you can adjust the difficulty on the fly with minor modifications or by simply increasing or
decreasing the level of effort you put into each reptition. Start with the lower body focused HIIT section to
to really get your hear and lungs working. Doing our usual Tabata style times intervals you can push yourself to
the max again znd agaoin with micro rest breaks. Then, tax your upper body with an intense round of cardio
kickboxing. Utilizing the same interval pattern as the high intensity interval training section we are able to really
wear out upper body without getting so tired that you want to give up. The key is to push yoursekf hard for
short bursts so you side in and out of aerobic and anaerobic training, getting the best of both worlds.

After a combined 16 minutes of intense cardio we drop the intensity down a bit for a core section desgined to
hit all the major muscles from hips to rib cage and many more. At 45 seconds a piece those abs should be
getting a nice burn by the time we are done. Then we finish off with a nice cool down stretch to help loosen up
any tight muscles before you go about the rest of your day.

Remeber that how much energy you burn is directly related to how much energy you put into each repition
so be sure to constantly push yourself to your comfortable maximum. Form is still the most important part of
any exercise so be sure to only push as hard as your form will allow.

Looking for support and motivation? Have unanswered fitness questions? Connect with the most helpful and
encouraging fitness community on the web in the Fitness Blender Community Forums.

Workout Structure:

- 3 Sections (HIIT, Kickboxing, Core)

- Each Section Uses a Times Interval

- Section 1 & 2 Use a Tabdata Interval

- Section 3 Uses 45 Sec On and 15 Off

Workout Structure:

- 3 Sections (HIIT, Kickboxing, Core)

- Each Section Uses a Timed Interval

- Section 1 & 2 Use a Tabata Internal

- Section 3 Uses 45 Sec Oen and 15 Off

Equipment:

- None Needed

Warm Up / Cool Down:

- Both Included

Printable HIIT Cardio kickboxing and Abs Workout:

Warm Up: 5 Min

- Side Step Arm Cross

- High Knee March

- Toe Touch Sweeps

- Squat Push Pull

- Toe Touch Kicks (L)

- Toe Touch Kicks (R)

- Sumo Squat w/Ventrak Raise

- Boxer Shuffle

- Up and Outs

- Jumping Jacks

HIIT: 8 Min (20 On, 10 off; Ab, Ab)

- Jumping Lunge

Burpee

- Lateral Jumps

- Walk Downs

- Squat Jack Jump (2 jacks then jump)

- Plank To Push Up

- Runner L

- Runner R

Kickboxing: 8 Min (20 On, 10 Off; AB, AB)

- Duck, Jab, Cross

- Uppercut, Jab, Cross, Knee

- Hook x2, Uppercut x2

- Uppercut x2, Hook x2, Knee

- Jab Cross

- Knee

Core: 8 Min

- Toe Touch Cross Crunch

- Back Bow Cross

- Double Side Leg Lift (L)

- Double Side Leg Lift (R)

- Plank

- Reverse Plank

- Side Plank (L)

- Side Plank (R)

Cool Down: 5 Min

- Seated Split + Arm Cross (L&R)

- Center Split Stretch

- Single Leg Toe Touch (L)

- Single Leg Toe Touch (R)

- Butterfly Stretch

- Torso Stretch (L)

- Torso Stretch (R)

- Cobra

- Shell Stretch

- Full Body Stretch

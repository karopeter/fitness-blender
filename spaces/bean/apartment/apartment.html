Apartment-friendly workouts that pack a
punch: workout videos for apartments & small
spaces

W hen I was a (completely & totally broke) college student and for a few years after, I lived in
small apartments. I was still mostly doing gym workouts at that point, but every now and again I
would tackle a workout in my apartment. That lasted until I dropped my rent off at the apartment manager's
office, and they asked, "Do you dance arount at night...or something? Because the neighbors downstairs are
wondering what it is you're doing up there."

It's important to be considerate of your neighbors. You never know who might need to wake up early for
work or who might be sleeping before they tackle a night shift or have a new baby or just want to get some
quite time without the pounding of someone's feet overhead. That's fair. But it also seems a fair objective to
want to be able to work out in your own living space - it's a seriously convenient, excuse-busting, money-
saving option. In an effort to keep both you and your downstairs neighbors happy. I've built a list of apartment
friendly workout videos that take into considereation the impact of landing and the amount of space you might
have available. These same routines may be great choices for hotel room workouts, too!

We also have over 500 workout videos that could also be easily modified to work for small spaces and
neighbors below (never hesistate to modify an exercise to make it work for your needs).

Strength Training

Lifting is a great option for apartment workouts because they don't require any jumping; you can get in a great
workout that builds muscle and makes you sweat, with almost no impact. All you need is a set of dumbbells or
resistance bands and an optional exercise mat. I favor dumbbells, but resistance bands are cheaper and take
up even less space. In terms of dumbbells, these adjustable PowerBlocks are spendly, but are well built and
take uo very little room (becuase of the way they fit together, the entire set has the footprint of a single large
dumbbell).

◇ Lower body focus: 40 minuted Build a Botty Workout - Strength and Pilates Workout for Butt and Thighs

◇ Upper body focus: 35 minuted Upper Body and Abs Workout - Compound Upper Body Workout for Strength
and Coordination

◇ Core & cardio focus: 39 Minuted Low Impact Cardio and Abs Workout with Warm Up and Cool Down

Need something shorter? Here are 10 minute versions of all the same muscle groups:

◇ Lower body focus: 10 Minute Butt and Thigh Workout - No Equipment Lower Body Workout

◇ Upper body focus: Fun Upper Body Workout + A Message About Goal Setting

◇ Core focus: 10 Minute Abs Workout - At Home Abs Workout with No Equipment

Apartment-Friendly Cardio Workouts

Cardio workouts may seem like an extra challenge, but the trick is to choose low impact cardio workouts that
don't involve jumping. That way, you can work up a good sweat without bothering neigbors. Here are a few
options that you can choose from, based on how much time you might have to commit to exercise today:

◇ 10 Minute Low Impact Cardio Workout for Beginners - Feel Good Cardio

◇ 26 Minute Low Impact Beginner Cardio Workout - Feel Good Recovery Cardio

Pilates Workouts

Pilates is a great way to get in a quiet workout that also uses a small workout space. None of these workouts
involve jumping, but they're all great for increasing range of motion, flexibility, and overall body awareness.

◇ 10 Minute Abs Workout - Pilates Abs Burner

◇ 20 Minute Pilates Workout: Pilates Butt and Thigh Workout

◇ 35 Minute Abs and Lower Body Pilates Workout for Beginners

◇ 62 Minute Pilates Abs, Butt and Thigh Workout - Pilates Workout for Lower Body & Core

Yoga/Stretching

Always important, yoga and/or stretching can be done nearly anywhere without bothering anyone else. These
workouts are slow, calm, and relaxing - and can be a great way to start your day, or end it. All of these also feel
great on muscles that are sore from previous workouts. Whether you're looking to gain flexibility or just mellow
out, thses stretching workouts won't bother your neighbors and they're easy workouts to do in hotel rooms as
well.

◇ 9 Minute Chill Out! Stretching, Pilates, Yoga Workout for Felxibility and Calm

◇ 18 Minute Lower Back Stretches for Stiff Sore Muscles

◇ 3 Day Flexibility Challenge Day 1: Fluid Full Body Stretches for Flexibility & Stress Relief

Hopefully you'll find a workouts and training styles on this list that will allow you to fire up your muscles and
your lungs, without getting an eviction notice.
